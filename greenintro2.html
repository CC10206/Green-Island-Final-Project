Introduction · Ecological Overview

Where the Kuroshio Meets: An Ecological Ark in the Pacific

Nurtured by the Kuroshio Current, this volcanic island has emerged as a premier hotspot for marine biodiversity in Taiwan.

Ecosystems
Sea turtles, coral communities, Tsuda's giant stick insects, coconut crabs.
Geological Landscapes
Volcanic terrain, pristine coastal forests, outdoor geology museum.
Natural Resources
One of only three submarine hot springs worldwide; a world-class natural marvel.
  • Biodiversity Hotspot: From the deep blue to the emerald forest.
  • Research Value: A natural laboratory of volcanic forests.
  • Rare Global Asset: One of the world's three saltwater hot springs.

Ongoing Crises · Conservation Status

Four Threats Pushing Green Island's Ecosystems to the Brink

Coral Bleaching
Rising sea temperatures have triggered mass bleaching in over 30% of surveyed reefs, leading to potential permanent coral loss across the island.
Taiwan Fruit Bat
Habitat loss from deforestation and urban expansion has caused a steady decline in the population of this vital endemic pollinator.
Wildlife Roadkill
High vehicle traffic during peak seasons poses a fatal threat to endemic species, especially during critical seasonal migrations.
Tourism Waste
Inadequate infrastructure struggles with the tourist load, as plastics and chemicals degrade reef habitats and contaminate local groundwater.
  • Coral Bleaching: Repeated thermal events impair reef recovery year-on-year.
  • Roadkill: Seasonal vehicle surges threaten crabs, snakes, and ground-nesting birds.
  • Visitor Capacity: Carrying-limit policies are still nascent; community stewardship is growing.
