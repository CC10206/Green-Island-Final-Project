Wuyouku · Green Island

Wuyouku is a breathtaking natural lagoon located on the northern side of the Green Island Lighthouse, formed by a protective ring of coral reefs. Known for its mirror-like waters and pristine white sand, it stands as one of the island's most iconic intertidal landscapes and a serene haven for visitors.

Natural Serenity and Tide Pools

The lagoon is shielded from the open sea's waves by a natural reef barrier, ensuring that the water remains tranquil regardless of the tide. During low tide, it transforms into a crystal-clear natural swimming pool, allowing travelers to safely observe the vibrant intertidal biodiversity up close.

The Double Reflection Photography

Wuyouku is the only spot on the island where photographers can capture the famous "Double Reflection" of the Green Island Lighthouse. When the winds are calm at low tide, the surface acts as a perfect mirror, reflecting the pure white tower against the blue Pacific sky, creating a stunning visual symmetry.
