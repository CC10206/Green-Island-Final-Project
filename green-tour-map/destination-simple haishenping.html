Haishenping · Green Island

Located on the eastern coast of Green Island, Haishenping is a breathtaking moon-shaped bay formed by a collapsed volcanic crater. It provides the most iconic vantage point on the island, beautifully showcasing dramatic volcanic geology and raw coastal beauty.

The Guardians of the Bay

Looking down from the ridges of the "Little Great Wall" trail, visitors are treated to a panoramic view of the famous Pekingese Dog and Sleeping Beauty rock formations. These remarkable basalt structures, sculpted by centuries of wave erosion, stand as silent guardians over the crystal-clear waters of the collapsed volcanic crater.

A Sanctuary for Stargazing

Facing the vast Pacific Ocean away from any light pollution, Haishenping offers a premier location for capturing the island’s nocturnal beauty. At night, the dark volcanic coastline provides a dramatic frame for Milky Way photography, inviting travelers to connect deeply with Green Island's timeless natural heritage under a blanket of stars.
