Eco Echo

Youzihu

柚子湖

Located in a sweeping coastal basin on the eastern shore, Yuzihu is an abandoned traditional village nestled inside a collapsed volcanic crater. Despite its name implying a lake, this historical valley is bounded by dramatic sea-eroded cliffs and the open Pacific, serving as a poignant time capsule of the island's earliest human settlements.

The Coral Stone Heritage

The village is renowned for its remnants of traditional coral stone houses, crafted by early settlers using sun-bleached coral clusters and volcanic rock. These weathered, roofless structures blend seamlessly into the rugged landscape, offering a tactile connection to Green Island's past and showcasing an ancient, sustainable harmony between pioneer adaptation and maritime nature.

Echoes of an Island Past

Surrounded by towering basalt walls, Yuzihu acts as a natural theater where the whispers of the wind through the ruins meet the crashing waves. As a cornerstone of the "Green Island · Continued Words" narrative, this quiet valley invites travelers to step off the beaten path, offering a profound space to contemplate the cycles of human history and the vital importance of cultural preservation.
