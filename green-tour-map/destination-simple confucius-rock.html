孔子岩

Confucius Rock · Green Island

Rising dramatically from the eastern coastline of Green Island, Confucius Rock is a striking sea-eroded pillar sculpted by centuries of marine forces. This solitary geological formation stands gracefully along the scenic ring road, capturing the imagination of passing travelers with its deeply symbolic silhouette.

The Philosopher in Stone

The rock is celebrated for its remarkable shape, which perfectly resembles the revered ancient sage, Confucius, standing in quiet contemplation. From a specific angle on the coastal road, the basalt column mimics a figure dressed in traditional flowing robes, bowing slightly toward the cliffside as if immersed in timeless meditation before the vast Pacific Ocean.

A Volcanic Masterpiece

As a product of the island's ancient volcanic history, the pillar showcases the incredible sculpting power of wind and wave erosion. Serving as a captivating roadside landmark, Confucius Rock invites travelers to slow down and appreciate how the raw forces of nature have crafted a piece of living art along Green Island’s rugged eastern highway.
